## Key decisions and findings

### Valuation with granted patent + working platform

Three scenarios produced (no brief written — verbal advisory):

- **Lower bound £500K–£2M** — IP-first, thin traction, acqui-hire or patent licence
- **Realistic £5M–£25M** — B2B route (law firms, wealth managers), some paying
  customers, patent granted and uncontested

  for IP scarcity; requires scale or active bidder competition

**Without consumer traction** (revised): lower and realistic scenarios compress but
don't collapse — IP licensing alone (£500K–£3M) and B2B first (£2M–£8M realistic)
are viable without winning the consumer marketing battle.

**Interested party types identified:** Apple (privacy brand), Google (Photos adjacent),
Microsoft (Azure compliance), 1Password/Bitwarden (digital legacy expansion),
DocuSign/Adobe (document escrow), LegalZoom/Farewill (estate planning), UK wealth
managers, custodian banks.

### Patent attorney fee reduction options

Three approaches assessed:
1. **Hybrid cash + equity** — reduced cash (£5K–£6K) + 0.5–1% equity vesting on
   grant. Possible with CIPA sole practitioners; unlikely at large firms.
2. **Shop to boutique/sole practitioners** — 30–40% cheaper than large firms;
   £6K–£9K realistic for a well-documented application.
3. **Priority filing first** — establishes priority date for £1K–£2K; buys 12 months
   to raise full fees. Does not prevent third-party implementation — LEG-006 tasked
   to clarify exact protection scope.

### JUXT investment options

Jon Pither (JUXT CEO) identified as a potential investor or strategic partner.
Four structures assessed:

1. **Jon personally via SEIS** — most tax-efficient (50% income tax relief). Available
   because Jon has no connection to Heirlooms Digital Ltd. Best route if Jon is
   comfortable with personal investment.
2. **JUXT Ltd straight equity** — no SEIS relief. Clean but makes Bret's employer
   a shareholder — changes the relationship permanently.
3. **Convertible loan note** — sidesteps valuation negotiation; converts at next round
   at a discount. Suitable if Jon wants upside without pricing the company now.
4. **Services for equity** — JUXT developer time in exchange for shares. Doesn't
   raise cash for patent filing. Useful as a parallel arrangement.

**Recommended sequencing:** Get JUXT outside interests consent (LEG-005) before any
investment conversation. Investment conversation is strongest after patent is filed —
Bret is then offering equity in a company with a defensible IP position, not asking
for funding to file.

**Most valuable JUXT contribution may not be cash** — an introduction to a law firm
or financial services client as the first institutional custodian costs Jon nothing
and opens the B2B route.

### Priority application protection scope

Clarified that a priority application establishes a priority date but does NOT prevent
third-party implementation. Enforcement rights arise only on patent grant. During the
gap (filing → grant, typically 2–4 years), trade secret protection (keeping repo
private) is the principal defence. LEG-006 tasked to Legal for precise mechanics.
